📡 The Radar — directions to watch

Each: what it is, who's doing it, and why it matters to you.

Agents that talk to each other (A2A) NEW LAYER

An open standard lets agents built by different vendors discover and call one another — forming an "agent mesh," with registries that act like a directory of approved agents.

Who: A2A protocol (now under the Linux Foundation) — 150+ organisations, live across Google, Microsoft, AWS; used in finance, insurance, supply chain.

Why it matters to you: MCP gateways connect agents to tools; A2A connects agents to other agents. That's the next plumbing layer — easy to miss if you stopped at MCP. source

Agentic core-banking, not chatbots

Agents that run whole back-office chains: spot a suspicious transaction → cross-check fraud patterns → check the rulebook → draft the compliance report → route it to the right officer, logging every step.

Who: HSBC, Citi, UBS, DBS, ING — reported 20–40% cost cuts and 10–30% revenue uplift in these workflows.

Why it matters to you: the real money is in autonomous operations (fraud, KYC, reconciliation, regulatory reporting), not customer chat. source

Gulf sovereign, Arabic-first models YOUR REGION

Home-grown large models built for Arabic (formal + Gulf/Levantine dialects), not just translated Western models.

Who: Falcon Arabic & Falcon-H1 (TII, UAE); Jais (G42 / MBZUAI / Cerebras); Saudi PIF's HUMAIN.

Why it matters to you: a FAB-relevant path — regional, compliant, dialect-aware models for local banking instead of depending only on US labs. source

India's consent-based data rails + AI

A national "Account Aggregator" network lets customers share bank/tax/insurance data with consent; AI then underwrites loans on this richer, permissioned data.

Who: Indian banks/lenders — reportedly 15–25% better default prediction; 90%+ coverage expected by 2027.

Why it matters to you: a different model — shared data infrastructure feeding AI — that the Gulf and others may copy. Watch it as a template, not just a local story. source

"Human-on-the-loop" (not in-the-loop)

A shift in posture: agents run on their own, people supervise via dashboards and alerts, stepping in only for exceptions — rather than approving every single step.

Who: emerging across enterprise agent platforms as the default oversight model.

Why it matters to you: it changes the operating model and the org chart — fewer approvers, more monitors. A governance mindset shift, not a tool. source

The "AI factory" operating model

A repeatable, full-stack setup (data + infrastructure + governance + workflow) so the firm ships many AI use-cases the same controlled way — sold as a package now.

Who: NTT DATA + NVIDIA "AI factories", IBM's AI operating-model blueprint. Yet only ~12% of firms have redesigned workflows at scale.

Why it matters to you: the winners treat AI as an operating-model redesign, not a pile of pilots. The gap between leaders and laggards is widening here. source

The counter-trend: agent rollbacks ⚠️

After the rush, many firms are pausing, trimming or quietly dropping agents once cost, risk and unclear value get scrutinised.

Who: reported across enterprises (one widely-cited figure: ~74% rolling back some agents after launch).

Why it matters to you: the honest picture — don't chase hype. Pick a few high-value workflows, prove ROI, then scale. Being second but measured can beat being first and burned. source
