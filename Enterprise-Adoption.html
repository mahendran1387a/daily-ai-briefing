📡 The radar

Each: what it is, who's doing it (with numbers), and why it matters to you.

Banks open their core to external agents NEW

Instead of building one chatbot, banks are exposing their platforms via MCP (Anthropic's open standard) so that the client's own AI agent can call the bank's data and workflows directly — like an API for autonomous software, not humans.

Who: Morgan Stanley is opening ShareWorks & Equity Edge — $1.2T in stock-plan assets, 3,400 corporate clients — to external AI agents via MCP, broader rollout through 2027 (CNBC, 3 Jun).

Why it matters to you: the question shifts from "do we have a copilot?" to "are our APIs/MCP servers ready for clients' agents to call us?" — a sales channel and a risk surface at the same time. CNBC
Hour-long autonomous agents are shipping NEW

The bar for "autonomy" jumps from minutes to hours: an agent now takes one goal and works through it across systems for an hour or two, only checking in for exceptions — what banks have been waiting for to put real money behind the technology.

Who: JPMorgan's chief analytics officer Derek Waldron (CNBC, 9 Jun): "We will have those in 2026." Overnight market-screening agents have already lifted private-banking gross sales by ~20%.

Why it matters to you: your operational risk teams need a clear answer on supervision & rollback for an agent that runs unattended for an hour — yesterday's review process won't fit. CNBC
Model access is now a sanctions question NEW

For the first time, the US government forced a frontier lab to shut off a publicly-launched model worldwide three days after release — model choice is no longer purely commercial; it's an export-control bet.

Who: US Commerce Dept's BIS sent Anthropic an "Is Informed" letter on 12 Jun for Claude Fable 5 & Mythos 5; Anthropic disabled both globally within hours because it can't filter non-US users in real time (Fortune, 13 Jun).

Why it matters to you: single-vendor model strategies just got riskier for a UAE bank — a US directive can pull your stack overnight; multi-model gateways and regional sovereign options move from "nice to have" to "must have". Fortune
"Agent-first" core banking from vendors

Core-banking suppliers are embedding AI agents directly into the systems banks already run — the agent assembles evidence across core systems and drafts the case, with humans reviewing.

Who: FIS + Anthropic launched a Financial Crimes AI agent (4 Jun) that cuts AML investigations from hours to minutes; BMO and Amalgamated Bank are piloting, GA in H2 2026.

Why it matters to you: your existing vendors are shipping agents into AML, onboarding and fraud — the buying decision shifts to governance and data control. FIS / Business Wire
Tiered agent governance is the next playbook NEW

Treating all agents with the same controls breaks both ways: simple agents get over-restricted, autonomous ones get under-restricted. The fix is rating agents by autonomy level and applying controls to match — read-only assistant vs. money-moving agent get different guardrails.

Who: Gartner (26 May): 40% of enterprises will demote or decommission autonomous agents by 2027 because they used uniform governance — incidents only show up in production.

Why it matters to you: a single agent-risk policy will fail audit; your AI governance committee needs an autonomy-tier model (read / advise / act / act-with-money) before scaling. Gartner
Gulf sovereign AI — your backyard YOUR REGION

The Gulf is building its own AI stack — sovereign operating models and regional compute that don't depend on US or Chinese infrastructure.

Who: Stargate UAE (G42, OpenAI, Oracle, Nvidia, Cisco, SoftBank) — 1GW, $30B+, first 200MW block due Q3 2026; G42 is also deploying an 8-exaflop Cerebras supercomputer in India (15 May); Saudi HUMAIN plans ~1GW of AMD+Nvidia compute.

Why it matters to you: a FAB-relevant path — compliant, regional infrastructure and models for local banking, not just US labs. The export-control episode makes this case stronger. G42
Agentic operations, not chatbots

Agents that run whole back-office chains: detect a suspicious transaction → write the blocking rule → draft the report → route it, logging every step.

Who: Commonwealth Bank's in-house fraud agent (80M+ signals/day) cut fraud losses >20% in H1 FY26; HSBC, Citi, UBS, DBS and ING report 20–40% cost cuts in such workflows.

Why it matters to you: the real return is autonomous operations (fraud, KYC, reconciliation, reporting), not customer chat. CommBank newsroom
The counter-trend: quality over speed ⚠️

After the rush, firms are pausing or trimming agents once cost, risk and unclear value get scrutinised — adoption is wide (88% of orgs use AI) but agent deployment is still in single digits per business function (Stanford HAI 2026 Index).

Who: Klarna remains the marquee reversal — it ran AI-only support then rehired humans for complex cases after quality slipped; the EU has also pushed its AI Act high-risk deadline from Aug 2026 to Dec 2027 (Digital Omnibus, 7 May) — buying banks 16 more months.

Why it matters to you: don't chase hype — pick a few high-value workflows, prove ROI with hard metrics, then scale. Stanford HAI
