📡 The radar

Each: what it is, who's doing it (with numbers), and why it matters to you.

Agents that talk to each other (A2A) NEW LAYER

An open standard lets agents from different vendors discover and call one another — an "agent mesh," with registries acting like a directory of approved agents.

Who: the A2A protocol, now hosted by the Linux Foundation — 150+ organisations, in production across Google, Microsoft and AWS.

Why it matters to you: MCP connects agents to tools; A2A connects agents to other agents — the next plumbing layer, easy to miss if you stopped at MCP. Linux Foundation
Watching the agents — eval & observability HOTTEST SPEND

Tools that test agents before launch and watch them in production — needed because models give unpredictable outputs normal tests miss.

Who: Gartner calls eval/observability the #1 production blocker (64%); agent-software spend is forecast at ~$206.5B in 2026, yet only ~17% of firms have deployed agents.

Why it matters to you: in a bank the budget goes to controlling agents before scaling them — this is where the money and the risk actually are. Gartner
Agentic core-banking, not chatbots

Agents that run whole back-office chains: detect a suspicious transaction → write the blocking rule → draft the report → route it, logging every step.

Who: Commonwealth Bank's in-house fraud agent (80M+ signals/day) cut fraud losses >20% in H1 FY26; HSBC, Citi, UBS, DBS, ING report 20–40% cost cuts in such workflows.

Why it matters to you: the real return is autonomous operations (fraud, KYC, reconciliation, reporting), not customer chat. CommBank newsroom
Gulf sovereign AI — your backyard YOUR REGION

The UAE is building its own AI stack — Arabic-first models plus sovereign cloud that doesn't depend on US or Chinese compute.

Who: the Central Bank of the UAE launched the world's first sovereign financial-cloud (built with Core42, a G42 company) in Feb 2026; Falcon (TII) and Jais (G42) are flagship models; Microsoft invested $1.5B in G42.

Why it matters to you: a FAB-relevant path — compliant, regional infrastructure and models for local banking, not just US labs. Gulf Business
India's consent-based data rails + AI

A national "Account Aggregator" network lets customers share bank/tax/insurance data with consent; AI then underwrites loans on this richer, permissioned data.

Who: Indian banks/lenders — reportedly 15–25% better default prediction; 90%+ coverage expected by 2027.

Why it matters to you: a different model — shared data infrastructure feeding AI — that the Gulf and others may copy. Watch it as a template. OplInnovate
"Human-on-the-loop" (not in-the-loop)

Agents run on their own; people supervise via dashboards and alerts, stepping in only for exceptions — rather than approving every step.

Who: emerging as the default oversight model across enterprise agent platforms, tied to the eval/observability build-out above.

Why it matters to you: it reshapes the org chart — fewer approvers, more monitors — and is a governance decision, not a tool purchase. ByteBridge
The counter-trend: agent rollbacks ⚠️

After the rush, firms are pausing or trimming agents once cost, risk and unclear value get scrutinised — quality, not speed, is the new test.

Who: Klarna is the marquee reversal — it ran AI-only support then rehired humans for complex cases after quality slipped.

Why it matters to you: don't chase hype — pick a few high-value workflows, prove ROI, then scale. Reuters/Bloomberg via Entrepreneur
