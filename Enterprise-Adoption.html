📡 The radar

Each: what it is, who's doing it (with numbers), and why it matters to you.

"Agent-first" core banking from vendors NEW

Core-banking suppliers are embedding AI agents directly into the systems banks already run — the agent assembles evidence across core systems and drafts the case, with humans reviewing.

Who: FIS + Anthropic launched a Financial Crimes AI agent (4 Jun) that cuts AML investigations from hours to minutes; BMO and Amalgamated Bank are piloting, GA in H2 2026.

Why it matters to you: you may not have to build agents in-house — your existing vendors are shipping them into AML, onboarding and fraud. The buying decision shifts to governance and data control. FIS / Business Wire
AI that hunts your security holes NEW

Frontier models are now used defensively — scanning codebases to find and help patch critical vulnerabilities before attackers (who have the same tools) get there.

Who: Anthropic's Project Glasswing flagged 10,000+ high/critical bugs in a month across 1,000+ projects; partners include JPMorgan, AWS, Google, Microsoft — expanded to ~150 orgs on 4 Jun.

Why it matters to you: AI-assisted security testing is becoming table stakes for banks; the bottleneck is now patching speed, not finding flaws. Anthropic
Watching the agents — eval & observability HOTTEST SPEND

Tools that test agents before launch and watch them in production — needed because models give unpredictable outputs normal tests miss.

Who: agent software spend is forecast at ~$206.5B in 2026 (up from $86.4B), but Gartner expects 40%+ of agentic projects cancelled by 2027 and says 88% of deployments report production incidents.

Why it matters to you: in a bank the budget goes to controlling agents before scaling them — this is where the money and the risk actually are. Gartner
Agentic operations, not chatbots

Agents that run whole back-office chains: detect a suspicious transaction → write the blocking rule → draft the report → route it, logging every step.

Who: Commonwealth Bank's in-house fraud agent (80M+ signals/day) cut fraud losses >20% in H1 FY26; HSBC, Citi, UBS, DBS and ING report 20–40% cost cuts in such workflows.

Why it matters to you: the real return is autonomous operations (fraud, KYC, reconciliation, reporting), not customer chat. CommBank newsroom
Gulf sovereign AI — your backyard YOUR REGION

The Gulf is building its own AI stack — sovereign operating models and regional compute that don't depend on borrowed US or Chinese infrastructure.

Who: Abu Dhabi's G42 launched 'Greenshield' + a Digital Embassies framework; Microsoft and G42 are adding 200MW of UAE capacity via Khazna; Saudi's HUMAIN plans ~1GW of AMD+Nvidia compute (~$10B+).

Why it matters to you: a FAB-relevant path — compliant, regional infrastructure and models for local banking, not just US labs. G42
"Human-on-the-loop" (not in-the-loop)

Agents run on their own; people supervise via dashboards and alerts, stepping in only for exceptions — rather than approving every step.

Who: emerging as the default oversight model across enterprise agent platforms, tied to the eval/observability build-out above.

Why it matters to you: it reshapes the org chart — fewer approvers, more monitors — and is a governance decision, not a tool purchase. ByteBridge
The counter-trend: quality over speed ⚠️

After the rush, firms are pausing or trimming agents once cost, risk and unclear value get scrutinised — and 88% of agent deployments report production incidents.

Who: Klarna is the marquee reversal — it ran AI-only support then rehired humans for complex cases after quality slipped.

Why it matters to you: don't chase hype — pick a few high-value workflows, prove ROI, then scale. Reuters/Bloomberg via Entrepreneur
