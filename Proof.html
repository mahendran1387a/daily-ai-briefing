Each story: what they adopted, the pain before, how they fixed it, the payoff, and what's next.

JPMorgan Chase

Largest US bank · biggest AI program in banking
Adopted
In-house AI assistant ("LLM Suite"), 450+ use cases, 2,000+ AI specialists.
Pain before
Huge manual document/contract work; knowledge scattered across the bank.
How fixed
Rolled the assistant to ~140,000 staff and automated contract review.
Payoff
~360,000 lawyer-hours saved a year; AI now core to an $18B tech budget.
Next
Moving from assistants to autonomous agents across more workflows. source

Bank of America

Consumer banking at massive scale
Adopted
"Erica" customer assistant + AI tools for nearly all employees.
Pain before
Overloaded call centres and IT help desks; slow developer workflows.
How fixed
Reached 90% employee AI adoption; Erica handles routine customer queries.
Payoff
3B+ interactions at 98% success; 50%+ fewer IT calls; 20%+ developer efficiency on a $4B bet.
Next
Deepening AI in customer service and developer tooling. source

Goldman Sachs

Investment banking · developer productivity
Adopted
AI coding agent ("Devin") + firmwide generative-AI tools.
Pain before
Developer time eaten by routine coding, upgrades and maintenance.
How fixed
Rolling the coding agent toward its ~12,000 developers to work alongside them.
Payoff
~3x developer productivity expected.
Next
Scaling "human + agent" teams across engineering. source

Citi & DBS Bank

Two more banks, two clear plays
Adopted
Citi: agents auto-handle software patches & upgrades. DBS: AI across customer + operations.
Pain before
Citi: slow, manual software maintenance. DBS: scaling service without scaling cost.
Payoff
Citi: developers freed from grunt work. DBS: ~$750M projected AI value.
Next
Both expanding from pilots to bank-wide agent use. source
