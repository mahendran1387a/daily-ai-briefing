JPMorgan Chase — "LLM Suite"

Firm-wide AI assistant · launched 2024, scaled through 2026
Built
A model-agnostic in-house assistant wired into the bank's own document stores, call records and knowledge graphs through secure internal APIs.
How it works
Routes prompts to OpenAI and Anthropic models behind the firewall; refreshed roughly every 8 weeks; rolled out opt-in.
Results
200,000 users in 8 months (now 230,000+); staff report 30–40% efficiency gains; contract review −40% in commercial lending; pitch decks drafted in ~30 seconds.
The catch
It's an assistant, not autonomy — value hinges on staff actually adopting it and on clean internal data; underwritten by an $18B tech budget few can match.
Next
Toward 1,000+ use cases and more autonomous agents. The Digital Banker · Tearsheet

Commonwealth Bank — agentic fraud defence

Australia's largest bank · in production 2026
Built
An agentic system that spots emerging fraud/scam patterns and auto-writes the detection rules to block them — built in-house in about 3 months.
How it works
Monitors 80M+ signals a day across transactions, cards, payments and digital channels, then drafts/updates fraud rules for review.
Results
Fraud losses down >20% (H1 FY26 vs FY25); ~40,000 proactive customer alerts/day; the agent contributed to ~75% of card-fraud rules.
The catch
Part of a $1B/yr financial-crime program — and it runs alongside a separate ~$1B suspected loan-fraud probe; scale invites scrutiny and needs human rule-review.
Next
Extending agentic detection to more channels. CommBank newsroom · Finextra

Klarna — the cautionary tale

Fintech · AI-only support, then a partial reversal
Built
An OpenAI-built assistant that replaced ~700 customer-service roles, handling high-volume chat in 35+ languages.
Results
Peaked at ~75% of chats (~2.3M conversations), 82% faster responses, 25% fewer repeat contacts.
The catch
Quality slipped. The CEO admitted Klarna "over-rotated" on AI; by 2025 it began rehiring humans for premium and complex cases.
Next
A blended model — AI for volume, humans for premium. The lesson: measure quality, not just cost. Reuters/Bloomberg via Entrepreneur

Goldman Sachs — autonomous coding

Investment bank · developer productivity
Built
Deploying Cognition's autonomous coding agent "Devin" alongside ~12,000 developers, plus firm-wide generative-AI tools.
How it works
Devin takes routine engineering work — upgrades, migrations, boilerplate — as a junior teammate that developers review.
Results
~3–4x productivity expected on targeted tasks (firm-stated, early-stage).
The catch
An expectation, not an audited outcome yet — human review still required, and gains concentrate on routine work, not complex design.
Next
Scaling "human + agent" engineering pods. Lucidate

The market reality — Menlo Ventures benchmark

Survey of ~500 enterprise decision-makers · context, not a single firm
Finding
Enterprise AI spend hit $37B in 2025 (3x year-on-year). Coding/dev tools led at $7.3B — about half of developers now use AI daily. AI apps are now ~6% of all software spend.
Why it matters
Separates real budget from hype: the money is concentrating in coding, industry-specific tools and copilots — not generic ML demos.
